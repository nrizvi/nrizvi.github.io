Autism is multi-dimensional and can be understood in a variety of ways. Some may use the medical model to define autism as a disorder, but this belief contributes to the marginalization we face in society. In contrast, 87% of autistic adults in the US view it as an identity. According to this, autism is a distinct neurotype, or unique way of thinking or expressing our thoughts.

Neurodiversity is a term used to describe all the diverse ways our brains can differ from each others' and also includes other cool identities such as ADHD, dyslexia, dyscalculia, and many more!

Unfortunately, robotics research fails to include these diverse perspectives.
